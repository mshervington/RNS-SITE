Welcome to the Re-Entry Navigation Service

When someone returns to New York City after surviving imprisonment in the New York State prison system (NYS Department of Corrections and Community Supervision, or DOCCS), they will often encounter people and agencies supposedly offering them help to successfully re-enter and remain in the free world. In reality, many such people and agencies are mainly interested in profiting from the returning citizen's need, usually by requiring their victims to go through "programs" such as "Moral Reconation Therapy" (MRT) that only reward the agency with grants and donations and positive publicity without actually helping the returnee to fulfill their parole release conditions, find a job, and obtain housing. These agencies and individuals merely prey on returning citizens through empty promises in their time of need.

The Re-Entry Navigation Service was created to prevent these abuses and help returning citizens successfully adapt to real-world life with practical insight from lived experience, and direct assistance, if needed, in accessing the resouces they need.
